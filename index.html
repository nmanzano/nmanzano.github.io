Tutti

Tutti is chat client app that was designed and developed in just one week. Collaboration between Adrain Thomas and myself, this was a project that we presented to our fellow classmates during our time at Galvanize. We both shared a common interest in developing a mobile app however we were undecided on whether to develop for iOS or Android, so we split the difference and developed in react native. This allowed us the option to build on both platforms.

Learning the react native framework was outside the core curriculum of Galvanize, however we felt well prepared as we were entering our 4th month and had a good understanding of JavaScript at this point. Never the less we were still working tirelessly for 7 days to reach MVP. During this time we were met with plenty of ups and downs.

Through out this project we found many wins, most notably was how well we collaborated. We spent the entire week glued to the hip, making sure we spoke through blockers that we had ran into while developing. Most importantly we encouraged each other, as it was easy to slip into a low moral due to stress of the time constraints. We reminded the other to take needed breaks and step away when stressed or exhausted. We white boarded problems that individually we couldnâ€™t solve.

Along with learning react native we also decided to learn firebase for use of our database and authentication. When it came to authentication we set it up without issue but the database presented its own set of difficulties. We were able to push chat messages into the database by the 5th day but had not structured it to present the chats in order of delivery. We kept running into also sorts of structure issues and with less than two days to present we began to doubt ourselves. We decided to shift our focus on solving this particular problem.

In our all is lost moment with only 30 minutes left till we presented a nonworking Tutti app. We went for a brainstorming walk outside as we did throughout the week, it was our way of separating ourselves from the computer screen. It was during this walk we had cried out eureka! We came up with the concept of adding timestamps to messages to organize the data. We quickly implemented this theory and found success!
